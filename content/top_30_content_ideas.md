# Top 30 Content Ideas for 10M Audience

**Goal**: Reach 10 million aggregate audience through cricket bowling biomechanics content on Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and TikTok.

**Core differentiator**: Skeleton overlay with phase-by-phase color coding (green/yellow/red) burned into video — making elite-level biomechanical analysis visible to everyone.

**Key audience insights from research**:
- 97% of Indian consumers watch short-form daily; 650M monthly Shorts viewers in India alone
- Only 19% of fans aged 18-34 watch an entire match — 74% get sports content from social media
- Shares and saves (especially WhatsApp forwards) matter more than passive views
- Cricket content on Instagram gets 45% higher engagement than other sports
- Women's cricket content is a massively underserved niche (5.2B video views for Women's Cricket World Cup 2025)
- Hindi + English bilingual captions are the cheapest, highest-leverage localization move

## Tier 1: Highest Viral Potential (Ideas 1-10)

These ideas combine strong emotional hooks, celebrity names, and the skeleton overlay as something never seen before. Each has the widest possible audience (fans + bowlers + coaches).

---

### 1. "Why Bumrah's Action Shouldn't Work — But Does"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Freeze-frame skeleton overlay on Bumrah's delivery stride. Show the hyperextended elbow, the whip-like wrist snap, the abnormal front-arm path. Overlay angle measurements. Then show a "textbook" action side-by-side — and explain why Bumrah's generates MORE pace and backspin despite breaking every rule.

**Why it works**: Bumrah is the most discussed bowling action in world cricket. The "shouldn't work but does" framing creates cognitive dissonance — the most powerful hook in educational content. Every Indian cricket fan shares this. Every coach debates this.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need to source Bumrah footage + run skeleton overlay analysis)

---

### 2. "Your Bowling DNA vs. Dale Steyn"

**Format**: 15-30s Reel/Short
**Style**: Split-screen. Left: anonymous club bowler skeleton. Right: Steyn skeleton. Both color-coded phase by phase. The overlay reveals which phases match (green) and which diverge (red). End card: "Want to find YOUR bowling DNA match? Link in bio."

**Why it works**: The "compare yourself to a legend" psychology is irresistible. Every young bowler wants to know which pro they bowl like. The skeleton overlay makes the comparison credible and visual — not just some guy talking. This format is infinitely repeatable with different pros.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need club bowler footage + pro reference footage)

---

### 3. "The 1 Fix That Added 10 km/h"

**Format**: 15s Reel/Short (series format)
**Style**: Before skeleton (red highlight on one body part) -> text overlay "3 weeks later" -> After skeleton (green highlight). Speed estimation overlay shows the delta. One specific fix. One measurable result.

**Why it works**: This is the format someone screenshots and sends to their WhatsApp cricket group saying "bro, we need to try this." Concrete, measurable, actionable. The before/after skeleton overlay is the proof that makes it credible. Short enough to rewatch.

**Difficulty**: Hard (requires real before/after footage from actual users, takes weeks to produce authentically)

---

### 4. "Why Fast Bowlers Break Down (The Skeleton Doesn't Lie)"

**Format**: 45-60s Reel/Short
**Style**: Show skeleton overlays of bowling actions with injury-risk mechanics highlighted in RED — mixed action (back vs front-on conflict), excessive lateral trunk flexion, hyperextended knee on landing. Overlay stress zones on joints. End with "every fast bowler should see their skeleton before their body tells them."

**Why it works**: Fear is a powerful motivator. Every fast bowler, coach, and parent of a young bowler cares about injury prevention. The red zones on the skeleton create urgency. This targets a broad audience beyond just technique enthusiasts — it reaches health-conscious parents and physios who will share it.

**Difficulty**: Low-Medium (can use any bowling footage, injury biomechanics well-documented)

---

### 5. "6 Bowling Actions, 6 Skeletons — Spot the Fastest"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short (interactive/quiz format)
**Style**: Show 6 skeleton overlays of different bowling actions side by side (anonymized — no faces, just bones). Ask audience to guess which one is the fastest. Reveal speeds one by one. The fastest isn't the one that "looks" fastest — the one with the best hip-shoulder separation and front-leg brace wins.

**Why it works**: Interactive content gets 2-3x the comments. The quiz format stops the scroll. The skeleton-only view strips away everything except mechanics — a genuinely new way to see bowling. Comments section becomes a debate zone (algorithm fuel).

**Difficulty**: Medium (need 6 different bowling clips processed)

---

### 6. "Left Arm vs Right Arm: The Skeleton Truth"

**Format**: 20-30s Reel/Short
**Style**: Mirror two skeleton overlays — a left-armer (Starc/Boult) and a right-armer (Steyn/Cummins). Show that the mechanics are NOT simply mirrored. Highlight the asymmetries in hip rotation, shoulder alignment, and follow-through path.

**Why it works**: Every left-arm bowler has been told "just do what the right-armers do, but mirrored." This content proves that's wrong with visual evidence. It's counterintuitive (shareworthy), educational (saveworthy), and relevant to a huge subset of bowlers.

**Difficulty**: Medium

---

### 7. "I Analyzed 100 Club Bowlers. Here's What They ALL Get Wrong."

**Format**: 45-60s Reel, expandable to 3-5 min YouTube
**Style**: Montage of skeleton overlays from real club bowlers. Highlight the 3 most common errors with red overlay (typically: front arm collapses early, head falls away, back foot lands chest-on). Show what it should look like with a green overlay. Statistics overlay: "87 out of 100 bowlers had this problem."

**Why it works**: The "data from real people" angle is different from every other cricket coach on Instagram who gives opinions. The number "100" signals authority. Every club bowler watching thinks "do I do that?" — which drives saves and shares. This positions wellBowled as the brand that analyzed real data, not just talked about theory.

**Difficulty**: Hard (requires building a real dataset of analyzed bowlers — but this compounds over time)

---

### 8. "Malinga vs Bumrah vs Starc: The 3 Most Unusual Actions Decoded"

**Format**: 45-60s Reel/Short, expandable to 5-8 min YouTube
**Style**: Triple split-screen skeleton overlays synchronized at the moment of front-foot contact. Show how three "unorthodox" actions achieve pace through completely different mechanical strategies. Malinga's sling. Bumrah's whip. Starc's height leverage.

**Why it works**: Three massive names in one video. Comparison content is inherently engaging — it creates debate in comments. The skeleton overlay makes the comparison scientific rather than opinion-based. This gets shared in cricket forums and group chats.

**Difficulty**: Medium-Hard (synchronizing three actions requires precise timing work)

---

### 9. "What Coaches Can't See (But the Skeleton Shows)"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Show a bowling action at normal speed — looks fine. Then overlay the skeleton. Freeze at key moments. Reveal subtle issues invisible to the naked eye: 5-degree hip rotation deficit, slight head tilt, micro-bend in the front knee at release. Each highlighted in yellow/red.

**Why it works**: This is a direct challenge to traditional coaching — but in a respectful, "technology reveals what eyes can't" framing. Coaches share this because it validates the need for tools. Bowlers share it because it explains why they plateau despite coaching. The "hidden truth revealed" format is one of the most shareworthy structures on social media.

**Difficulty**: Low-Medium (single clip analysis with annotations)

---

### 10. "From Backyard to Biomechanics Lab in 30 Seconds"

**Format**: 15-30s Reel/Short
**Style**: Start with raw phone footage of a kid bowling in a backyard. Messy, casual, authentic. Then the wellBowled skeleton overlay materializes on top of the footage in real-time. Phase labels appear. Color coding lights up. Speed estimation overlay. The backyard transforms into a biomechanics lab. End card: "Elite analysis. Your phone. Free."

**Why it works**: The transformation is the hook. The contrast between casual backyard cricket and elite-looking analysis is visually stunning. This demonstrates the product while being entertaining. Parents share this. Young bowlers want to try it. The "free" angle removes all friction. This is the best brand-introduction video.

**Difficulty**: Low (film any kid bowling, run through the app)

## Tier 2: High Viral Potential (Ideas 11-20)

Strong content with slightly more niche appeal or higher production requirements. Still capable of millions of views with the right execution.

---

### 11. "Speed Estimation: How Fast Do You ACTUALLY Bowl?"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short (series)
**Style**: Film someone bowling. Before they see the result, ask them to guess their speed. Show the skeleton overlay computing the speed from biomechanics. Reveal the actual estimation. Reaction shot. Repeat with 3-4 bowlers per video.

**Why it works**: The "guess and reveal" format is proven across every content vertical. Every bowler overestimates their speed. The gap between perception and reality creates entertainment and relatability. Comment section fills with "do me next" requests.

**Difficulty**: Low (just need bowlers willing to be filmed + the app)

---

### 12. "Women's Fast Bowling: The Untold Biomechanics Story"

**Format**: 45-60s Reel, expandable to 5 min YouTube
**Style**: Skeleton overlay analysis of elite women fast bowlers (Shabnim Ismail, Darcie Brown, Megan Schutt). Show that women's bowling mechanics have distinct characteristics — often cleaner actions, different injury risk profiles. Celebrate technical excellence with data.

**Why it works**: Women's cricket content generated 5.2B video views at the 2025 World Cup, but almost zero biomechanics analysis exists. First-mover advantage in a massive, underserved market. Engagement rates are higher because the audience is hungry for serious technical content about women's cricket, not just highlight reels.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need to source women's cricket footage)

---

### 13. "POV: Your Coach Uses AI to Fix Your Action"

**Format**: 15-30s Reel/TikTok
**Style**: First-person perspective. Coach holds phone, films bowler, skeleton overlay appears in real-time. Coach points at a red zone, explains the fix in 10 seconds, bowler tries again, skeleton shows improvement (yellow -> green). Quick, authentic, no polish.

**Why it works**: POV format is native to TikTok/Reels. The raw, authentic feel outperforms polished content. This shows the product in a real coaching scenario — the most relatable use case. Coaches share this because it makes them look innovative. The "before -> after in one session" arc is satisfying.

**Difficulty**: Low (film at any training session)

---

### 14. "The Perfect Bowling Action Doesn't Exist (Here's Proof)"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Show skeleton overlays of 5 elite bowlers — all different, all successful. McGrath (metronomic), Shoaib (raw pace), Warne (spin mastery), Anderson (swing craft), Archer (simplicity). Overlay text: "There is no perfect action. There's only YOUR optimal action." End with DNA matching concept.

**Why it works**: Counterintuitive thesis ("perfection doesn't exist") challenges dogma. Every bowler who has been told their action is "wrong" feels validated. The DNA matching concept is the natural CTA — find YOUR optimal action.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need 5 different pro clips processed)

---

### 15. "Father-Son Bowling DNA: Do You Bowl Like Your Dad?"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Film a father and son bowling. Overlay both skeletons side-by-side. Highlight the inherited mechanics and the differences. Emotional music. End with their DNA match percentage.

**Why it works**: Pure emotional content. Every cricket-loving father will share this. The generational connection through cricket is deeply cultural in India, Australia, England, Pakistan. This is the content that gets saved, forwarded to family WhatsApp groups, and rewatched. Engagement through emotion, not just information.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need to find and film father-son cricket pairs)

---

### 16. "Rank These Bowling Actions (Comment 1-5)"

**Format**: 15-30s Reel/Short
**Style**: Show 5 skeleton overlay clips in sequence. No context, no names. Just skeletons bowling. Ask audience to rank them 1-5 in comments based on which looks most "effective." Reveal the bowlers and their career stats in a follow-up post.

**Why it works**: Interactive content drives massive comment counts. The skeleton-only view removes bias (no one can tell who the bowler is). The follow-up reveal drives return visits and profile follows. Two posts for the price of one analysis session.

**Difficulty**: Low-Medium (need 5 clips, simple editing)

### 17. "This 14-Year-Old Bowls Like Mitchell Starc"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Side-by-side skeleton overlay. Left: unknown teenage bowler. Right: Starc. Overlay highlights the matching mechanics — similar arm path, similar front-leg brace, similar follow-through angle. DNA match percentage appears: "87% Starc DNA."

**Why it works**: The "unknown talent discovered by AI" narrative is irresistible. Every young bowler and their parents want to believe they could be the next star. This is aspirational content that gets saved and shared widely. The specific age makes it relatable to the target demographic.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need to find a young bowler whose action genuinely matches)

---

### 18. "Spin vs. Pace: The Skeleton Tells a Completely Different Story"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Side-by-side skeleton overlay of a fast bowler and a spin bowler delivering simultaneously. The skeletons move in dramatically different ways — different energy sources, different timing, different stress points. Highlight in color: where a fast bowler loads (legs, core) vs. where a spinner loads (shoulder, wrist, fingers).

**Why it works**: Most cricket content separates pace and spin. Showing them together skeleton-on-skeleton is visually striking and educational. Appeals to both pace and spin bowlers — doubling the audience. The "two completely different sports" realization is the shareable insight.

**Difficulty**: Low-Medium

---

### 19. "How To Bowl 140+ km/h (The Biomechanics Checklist)"

**Format**: 45-60s Reel, expandable to 5-8 min YouTube
**Style**: Numbered checklist format. Each item illustrated with a skeleton overlay freeze-frame. 1. Run-up momentum transfer (hip speed at back-foot contact). 2. Bound height and distance. 3. Hip-shoulder separation angle (show the exact degrees). 4. Front-leg brace stiffness. 5. Release point height. Each item: green = you're doing it, red = you're losing pace here.

**Why it works**: "How to bowl fast" is the most searched cricket coaching query. Structuring it as a biomechanics checklist with skeleton overlay proof differentiates this from every talking-head coach video. The numbered format encourages saves. Bowlers will screenshot individual frames.

**Difficulty**: Medium

---

### 20. "Same Bowler, Fresh vs. Fatigued (Spell 1 vs. Spell 3)"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Overlay the same bowler's skeleton from their first over and their 15th over. Show how fatigue changes mechanics: arm drops, front knee buckles, head falls away, hip rotation decreases. Color overlay shifts from green to yellow to red as fatigue accumulates.

**Why it works**: Every fast bowler has experienced the "why am I slower in my third spell?" problem. This gives them the visual answer. Coaches use this to justify rotation strategies. The deterioration is dramatic and visually compelling. Highly saveworthy for anyone who coaches or plays.

**Difficulty**: Hard (need extended footage of same bowler across a match/session)

## Tier 3: Strong Niche Content (Ideas 21-30)

More specialized content that builds authority, serves the core community, and creates a library of reference material. Lower individual viral ceiling but higher conversion and loyalty.

---

### 21. "Bowling Action Evolution: Age 12 to Age 22"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel, expandable to 3 min YouTube
**Style**: Show skeleton overlays of the same bowler at different ages (if footage exists) or different bowlers at different development stages. Show how the action matures: run-up lengthens, bound increases, arm speed increases, but some faults persist from childhood. Track the DNA across time.

**Why it works**: Longitudinal content is rare and fascinating. Parents of young cricketers are deeply invested in their child's development. This format shows that improvement is a journey, not a switch — encouraging patience and long-term app use.

**Difficulty**: Very Hard (requires archival footage or years of data)

---

### 22. "The Most Dangerous Delivery in Cricket (Biomechanics of the Yorker)"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Skeleton overlay at the moment of yorker release vs. a length ball. Freeze-frame the difference: release point is later, wrist angle changes, follow-through is more aggressive. Show the exact biomechanical signatures that make a yorker a yorker.

**Why it works**: The yorker is the most iconic delivery in limited-overs cricket. Breaking down its biomechanics is educational and entertaining. Every fast bowler wants to bowl a better yorker. The "secret revealed by skeleton analysis" angle makes it feel like insider knowledge.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need clear yorker vs length-ball footage from same bowler)

---

### 23. "Net Session Mistakes: 5 Things You're Practicing Wrong"

**Format**: 45-60s Reel, expandable to 5 min YouTube
**Style**: Film in actual nets. Show skeleton overlays of common practice mistakes: bowling too many bouncers in nets (different mechanics than match bowling), not following through properly, bowling from a standing start instead of full run-up. Each mistake highlighted in red with the correct version in green.

**Why it works**: Every club cricketer practices in nets. Telling them they're doing it wrong gets attention. Showing the proof via skeleton overlay makes it actionable, not just opinionated. Coaches will share this to their teams before training sessions.

**Difficulty**: Low-Medium (film at any nets session)

---

### 24. "Country vs. Country: How Australian Bowlers Differ From Indian Bowlers"

**Format**: 45-60s Reel, expandable to 5 min YouTube
**Style**: Skeleton overlay comparison of typical Australian fast bowling mechanics (high arm, front-on, aggressive bounce) vs. Indian fast bowling mechanics (more side-on, wrist-heavy, seam-focused). Use 3-4 bowlers from each country to show patterns.

**Why it works**: Nationalism is the strongest engagement driver in cricket content. India vs. Australia is the biggest rivalry. This gives both sides something to debate in comments. The "bowling culture" angle is unique — nobody else has framed national differences through biomechanics data.

**Difficulty**: Hard (need multiple clips from each country, careful to avoid stereotyping)

---

### 25. "Can AI Detect a No-Ball Before the Umpire? (Skeleton Foot Tracking)"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short
**Style**: Skeleton overlay with foot-tracking highlight. Show the front foot landing relative to the crease. Overlay a line. Freeze-frame. Was it a no-ball? The skeleton analysis reveals the answer before the umpire's call. Test it against actual match decisions.

**Why it works**: Umpiring controversy drives massive engagement in cricket. The "AI vs. human" angle is timely and debatable. Comment sections will explode with opinions. This is the kind of content that gets picked up by cricket news accounts and reshared.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need clear footage showing front-foot landing)

---

### 26. "Tamil Commentary + Skeleton Breakdown"

**Format**: 30-45s Reel/Short (regional language series)
**Style**: Same skeleton overlay analysis but with Tamil/Hindi/Urdu commentary. Culturally specific references and humor. Target specific regional cricket communities. Start with Tamil (your strength), then expand.

**Why it works**: Regional language cricket content is driving growth in Tier-2 and Tier-3 Indian cities. There is almost zero biomechanics content in Tamil, Hindi, or Urdu. First-mover advantage in massive markets. WhatsApp sharing in regional language groups is the primary distribution channel.

**Difficulty**: Low (same analysis, different audio track)

---

### 27. "React to My Bowling Action" (User-Submitted Content Series)

**Format**: 30-60s Reel/Short
**Style**: User submits their bowling clip. You run the skeleton overlay, identify one strength and one area for improvement. Deliver the analysis in 30 seconds. Genuine reaction to what the skeleton reveals. End with "DM me your clip for a free analysis."

**Why it works**: User-generated content drives engagement loops. Every person whose clip is featured shares it across their network. The "DM me" CTA builds a submission pipeline — infinite content supply. The genuine reaction format feels authentic, not scripted.

**Difficulty**: Low (users provide the footage, you run analysis and react)

---

### 28. "The Injury That Changed Everything (Archer's Skeleton Story)"

**Format**: 45-60s Reel, expandable to 5-8 min YouTube
**Style**: Narrative format. Show Jofra Archer's pre-injury skeleton overlay: simple action, extreme pace, but stress points highlighted in red at the elbow. Explain how his biomechanics generated speed but at a structural cost. The skeleton predicted the injury risk before the injury happened.

**Why it works**: Storytelling + data is the highest-retention content format. Archer's injury saga is well-known and emotional. Framing it through biomechanics gives a fresh, authoritative angle. Parents and coaches share this because injury prevention is a universal concern.

**Difficulty**: Medium (need pre-injury Archer footage, careful narrative construction)

---

### 29. "Skeleton Overlay ASMR: The Perfect Bowling Action in Slow Motion"

**Format**: 15-30s Reel/TikTok
**Style**: No commentary. No text. Just a perfect bowling action in ultra-slow-motion with the skeleton overlay smoothly tracking every joint. Satisfying, hypnotic, visually beautiful. Stump mic audio: leather on pitch, boot on ground. Let the skeleton do the talking.

**Why it works**: "Satisfying" and ASMR content is one of the largest content categories across all platforms. The skeleton overlay in slow motion IS inherently satisfying — watching the human body move in a complex athletic motion, rendered as clean geometric lines. This reaches beyond cricket fans into the general "satisfying content" audience. Low effort, high reach potential.

**Difficulty**: Very Low (single clip, no editing beyond the overlay)

---

### 30. "Build Your Bowling Action From Scratch (The Skeleton Blueprint)"

**Format**: 60s Reel, expandable to 8-10 min YouTube
**Style**: Start with a static skeleton in T-pose. Animate it into each phase of a bowling action, one phase at a time. Run-up (hip drive). Gather (coil). Bound (elevation). Delivery stride (front-leg brace). Release (wrist snap). Follow-through (deceleration). Each phase gets its optimal angles and positions overlaid. Build the "ideal" action piece by piece.

**Why it works**: The "build from scratch" format is educational and satisfying. It works for beginners who are learning from zero and for experienced bowlers who want to understand the theory. The animated skeleton building up phase-by-phase is a genuinely novel visual that no one else produces. Coaches save and use this as a teaching reference.

**Difficulty**: Hard (requires custom animation or composite skeleton renders, not just overlay on existing footage)

## Cross-Cutting Rules for All 30 Ideas

1. **First frame must hook in under 1 second** — bold text overlay + freeze-frame skeleton
2. **Skeleton overlay is in EVERY video** — it is the brand signature, the visual moat
3. **One insight per video** — never try to teach three things
4. **English voice + Hindi subtitle minimum** — Tamil/Urdu as bonus tracks
5. **End every video with a soft CTA** — "Follow for daily bowling analysis" or "Link in bio for your free analysis"
6. **Film landscape, crop to 9:16 in post** — preserves quality, enables repurposing to 16:9 YouTube
7. **Post the same content to Reels + Shorts + TikTok** — the algorithm overlap is minimal, reach compounds
8. **Reply to every comment in the first hour** — algorithm fuel
9. **Batch-produce 5-7 clips per filming session** — efficiency at the source
10. **Track shares and saves, not views** — a clip shared to a WhatsApp group of 15 cricketers is worth 10,000 passive views
