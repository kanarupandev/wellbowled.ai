# Video Quality Checklist — Upload-Ready Standard

> Every video must pass ALL checks before posting.
> If it doesn't feel like broadcast TV analysis, it's not ready.

---

## A. RESOLUTION & FORMAT (7 checks)

- [ ] A1. Output is exactly 1080×1920 (9:16 portrait)
- [ ] A2. H.264 codec, MP4 container
- [ ] A3. 30fps constant frame rate (no variable frame rate)
- [ ] A4. Bitrate ≥ 6 Mbps (no compression artifacts on fast motion)
- [ ] A5. Duration between 15s and 60s (Instagram Reel sweet spot)
- [ ] A6. No black frames at start or end (clean first/last frame)
- [ ] A7. File size under 100MB (Instagram limit)

## B. FIRST FRAME / HOOK (6 checks)

- [ ] B1. First frame is visually striking — would you stop scrolling?
- [ ] B2. Hook text appears within first 0.5s (not after 2s)
- [ ] B3. Hook is readable at phone size (minimum 48px font equivalent)
- [ ] B4. No blank/black/loading frame at start — action begins immediately
- [ ] B5. The bowling action is visible in frame 1 (not a title card)
- [ ] B6. Color contrast: text is readable against the video background

## C. OVERLAY DESIGN — LINES & SKELETON (10 checks)

- [ ] C1. Hip line is clearly distinct from shoulder line (different hue, not similar)
- [ ] C2. Line colors pop against ALL backgrounds in the clip (test dark & light areas)
- [ ] C3. Line width is 3-5px at 1080p — thick enough to see, thin enough to be elegant
- [ ] C4. Lines extend 20-30% beyond joints — looks intentional, not clipped
- [ ] C5. Joint dots have white border (1.5-2px) — pops against any jersey color
- [ ] C6. Skeleton (if shown) is subtle — 30-40% opacity, NOT competing with the hip/shoulder lines
- [ ] C7. Lines track the bowler smoothly — no jitter, no frame-to-frame jumping
- [ ] C8. Lines disappear gracefully if landmarks lose visibility (fade out, don't snap off)
- [ ] C9. At peak separation, lines are maximally diverged and visually dramatic
- [ ] C10. No overlay elements obscure the bowler's face or key body parts

## D. TYPOGRAPHY & TEXT (9 checks)

- [ ] D1. Font is clean sans-serif (SF Pro, Inter, or Helvetica Neue — NOT Comic Sans, NOT serif)
- [ ] D2. All text has a dark pill/shadow behind it — readable on any background
- [ ] D3. Pill backgrounds are rounded (border-radius ≥ pill height / 2)
- [ ] D4. Pill opacity is 55-70% black — dark enough to read, transparent enough to see video
- [ ] D5. Angle numbers use monospace or tabular figures (digits don't jump width as they change)
- [ ] D6. Text size hierarchy: angle number (large) > phase label (medium) > legend (small)
- [ ] D7. No text overlaps other text
- [ ] D8. No text is cut off by screen edges (minimum 16px safe margin on all sides)
- [ ] D9. Text appears/disappears with subtle fade (100-200ms) — no hard snap on/off

## E. ANIMATION & MOTION (8 checks)

- [ ] E1. Slow-motion is smooth — no stuttering, no frame drops
- [ ] E2. Speed transitions (1x→0.25x) have a brief ramp (not instant cut to slow)
- [ ] E3. Freeze frame has a subtle pulse/flash when it locks (signals "pay attention")
- [ ] E4. Angle number animates smoothly as separation changes (interpolated, not jumping)
- [ ] E5. Phase label transitions use a slide or fade (not a hard cut)
- [ ] E6. "PEAK X-FACTOR" badge has an entrance animation (scale up + glow, 300ms)
- [ ] E7. End card fades in, not a hard cut
- [ ] E8. Overall pacing feels rhythmic — not too fast, not too slow, each segment breathes

## F. COLOR SCIENCE (7 checks)

- [ ] F1. Hip line: warm pink/magenta (#FF69B4 or similar) — reads as "hips/lower body"
- [ ] F2. Shoulder line: cool cyan/turquoise (#00CED1 or similar) — reads as "shoulders/upper body"
- [ ] F3. The two colors are on opposite sides of the color wheel (warm vs cool = instant distinction)
- [ ] F4. Separation angle number is white with dark shadow — readable on any background
- [ ] F5. Peak badge uses accent color (gold/yellow or brand peacock blue #006D77)
- [ ] F6. Verdict card uses brand dark background (#0D1117) with white text
- [ ] F7. No neon/garish colors that feel cheap — palette is premium sports broadcast quality

## G. COMPOSITION & LAYOUT (8 checks)

- [ ] G1. Bowler is centered or rule-of-thirds positioned — not awkwardly off to one side
- [ ] G2. If input is landscape, the portrait crop keeps the bowler in frame throughout
- [ ] G3. No important content in top 10% or bottom 10% of frame (Instagram UI covers these)
- [ ] G4. Angle number positioned near the torso (contextually placed, not floating randomly)
- [ ] G5. Phase label is top-center, consistently positioned across all frames
- [ ] G6. Legend bar is bottom of frame, outside the action area
- [ ] G7. At most 3-4 overlay elements visible simultaneously (not cluttered)
- [ ] G8. Negative space is respected — the video breathes, overlays don't fill every pixel

## H. PACING & STRUCTURE (8 checks)

- [ ] H1. Cold open (full speed, no overlay) is 1-2s max — just enough to show the raw action
- [ ] H2. Slow-mo analysis section is the longest segment (50-60% of total duration)
- [ ] H3. Freeze at peak separation holds for exactly 2-3s (long enough to read, short enough to not bore)
- [ ] H4. Verdict card is 2-3s max — punchy, not an essay
- [ ] H5. End card is 1s max — don't linger
- [ ] H6. Total video feels like it EARNS every second — nothing is filler
- [ ] H7. The "aha moment" (peak separation reveal) lands between 40-60% of the video
- [ ] H8. Rewatchability: a viewer should want to watch it again to catch details they missed

## I. CONTENT & INSIGHT (6 checks)

- [ ] I1. The insight is SPECIFIC — not "good action" but "47° separation at front foot contact"
- [ ] I2. A non-cricketer can understand the visual (two lines diverging = power generation)
- [ ] I3. A serious bowler learns something they can apply to their own training
- [ ] I4. The verdict text is actionable — "work on hip mobility" not just "needs improvement"
- [ ] I5. Famous bowler name (if applicable) appears early for discoverability
- [ ] I6. The video answers one question clearly: "Where does pace come from?"

## J. BRAND & POLISH (6 checks)

- [ ] J1. wellBowled.ai watermark is present — bottom corner, semi-transparent, not distracting
- [ ] J2. Color legend bar shows: pink = hips, cyan = shoulders (teaches the visual language)
- [ ] J3. Consistent visual style with all other wellBowled.ai content (same fonts, colors, pill style)
- [ ] J4. No visual artifacts — no encoding glitches, no half-rendered frames, no pixel noise
- [ ] J5. Feels premium — would not look out of place on an ESPN or Sky Sports broadcast
- [ ] J6. Bilingual caption line (English + Tamil/Hindi) if targeting India audience

## K. PLATFORM COMPLIANCE (5 checks)

- [ ] K1. Plays correctly on Instagram (test in preview before posting)
- [ ] K2. No copyrighted music (use original audio, ambient sound, or royalty-free)
- [ ] K3. No watermarks from other apps (CapCut, TikTok, etc.)
- [ ] K4. Thumbnail (first frame or custom) is compelling at small size
- [ ] K5. Caption/description drafted with relevant hashtags (#cricket #bowling #biomechanics)

## Quick Pre-Upload Ritual (5 seconds)

1. Watch it on your PHONE (not computer) — does it look good at phone size?
2. Watch it WITHOUT SOUND — does the visual alone tell the story?
3. Show the first frame to someone — do they stop or scroll?
4. Ask: "Would I send this to a mate who bowls?" — if no, it's not ready.
5. Ask: "Would ESPN use this graphic?" — if no, refine the overlays.
