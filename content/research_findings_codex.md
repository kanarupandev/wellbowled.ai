# wellBowled.ai Research Findings

Prepared by Codex for `/Users/kanarupan/workspace/wellbowled.ai/content`

Research window reviewed:

- January 9, 2025 to February 25, 2026 for platform, creator, and category trend signals
- June 27, 2018 for the latest ICC demographic baseline found in this research pass

## Document Purpose

This memo translates current platform trends, cricket audience signals, and creator-economy patterns into a focused content strategy for `wellBowled.ai`.

The point is not to produce general "social media content." The point is to build a durable content engine that:

- attracts serious bowlers, coaches, parents, and cricket fans
- proves the value of `wellBowled.ai` in public
- compounds into a recognizable media brand
- creates a top-of-funnel for app usage, academy partnerships, and community submissions

## What This Memo Answers

This document answers five practical questions:

1. What short-form content trends matter right now?
2. Which audience segments are most valuable for `wellBowled.ai`?
3. How should the brand position itself in public?
4. Which channels should be prioritized, and why?
5. What concrete content plan and operating model should be used in the next 90 days?

## Research Method

This pass prioritized primary sources:

- Meta / Instagram / Facebook official newsroom posts
- YouTube official blog and Culture and Trends reporting
- TikTok official newsroom reporting
- ICC official media releases and tournament reports
- Government of India Press Information Bureau notices for India-specific platform availability constraints

Where a recommendation goes beyond what a source states directly, it is marked as an inference.

## Executive Summary

The strongest current opportunity for `wellBowled.ai` is an India-first, creator-led, proof-heavy short-form strategy built primarily on Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts.

The core strategic thesis is:

- people do not want app demos first
- people want visible bowling insight first
- the app should appear as the proof engine behind the insight

The strongest content position for `wellBowled.ai` is not "AI app account." It is "specialist bowling coach and analysis brand powered by machine-backed proof."

The market signals point to six clear operating realities:

1. Reels is a dominant short-form surface in India.
2. Sharing, reposting, and DM behavior matter as much as raw view count.
3. Creator-led sports commentary is now mainstream, not fringe.
4. Strong first-second hooks are non-negotiable on Shorts and Reels.
5. Multilingual and highly visual content travels further.
6. Women's cricket is large enough and fast-growing enough to justify dedicated editorial focus.

The immediate recommendation is:

- prioritize Instagram Reels as the primary channel
- use YouTube Shorts as the second primary channel
- cross-post to Facebook Reels as a low-friction extension
- treat TikTok as optional unless there is a non-India distribution path
- lead with bowling diagnosis, technique proof, and progress content
- use the app as evidence, not as the entire story

## Strategic Thesis

`wellBowled.ai` can win attention if it behaves like a specialist media brand with product-backed proof.

That means:

- the hook is human and coaching-led
- the proof is visual and specific
- the product appears when it strengthens credibility

Weak public positioning:

- "Our AI analyzes your bowling"

Strong public positioning:

- "You are leaking pace here. Freeze the clip at release. This is the frame where your front side collapses, and this is how `wellBowled.ai` catches it."

## Current Market Signals

### Signal 1: Reels is an especially strong India-first discovery surface

Meta said on September 11, 2025 that 97% of surveyed Indian consumers watch short-form video daily, 92% preferred Reels over other surveyed short-form options, and Instagram Reels drove about 33% higher creator engagement than other surveyed platforms.

Source:

- [Five Years On, Reels Reigns as India's Top Short-Form Video Platform](https://about.fb.com/news/2025/09/five-years-on-reels-reigns-as-indias-top-short-form-video-platform/)

Why this matters:

- India-first consumer attention is already concentrated in short-form video.
- Reels has both reach and creator performance momentum.
- `wellBowled.ai` should treat Reels as the top-of-funnel engine, not an afterthought.

### Signal 2: Social sharing is now core distribution, not just a secondary behavior

Meta said on September 28, 2025 that Instagram had reached 3 billion monthly active users globally and that people reshare Reels more than 4.5 billion times per day across Meta platforms.

Meta also said on August 6, 2025 that it was adding reposts and a Friends tab in Reels so people can see content their friends liked, reposted, created, or commented on.

Sources:

- [In India, Instagram debuts a Reels-first experience for its mobile app](https://about.fb.com/news/2025/09/in-india-instagram-debuts-a-reels-first-experience-for-its-mobile-app/)
- [New Instagram Features to Help You Connect](https://about.fb.com/news/2025/08/new-instagram-features-help-you-connect/)

Why this matters:

- shareability is now a first-order metric
- the most valuable clips are the ones someone sends to a teammate, coach, or parent
- content should be built for private utility, not only public entertainment

### Signal 3: Shorts and Reels reward compact stories with immediate hooks

YouTube's January 28, 2025 Shorts deep dive emphasized that the first second matters and that Shorts should function as mini-stories rather than fragments cut from long videos.

Source:

- [YouTube Shorts deep dive](https://blog.youtube/creator-and-artist-stories/youtube-shorts-deep-dive/)

Why this matters:

- every clip needs a visible premise in the opening second
- bowling content must show the problem or promise immediately
- long setup kills retention in this category

### Signal 4: Creator-led sports commentary is mainstream

YouTube's Culture and Trends report published February 25, 2026 found that 66% of surveyed sports fans in Mexico ages 14 to 49 watch or engage with creator or fan sports commentary and analysis weekly or more, and 63% said they like creator-invented sports challenges and events.

Source:

- [New report shows impact of Latin American creators on sport's media landscape](https://blog.youtube/culture-and-trends/youtube-latin-america-football-trends-report/)

Important note:

### Signal 5: Multilingual and visual-first content has structural upside in India

Google India said on December 8, 2025 that 77% of surveyed Gen Z in India watched translated creators in the previous 12 months and 68% used language or slang picked up from digital video. The same post also highlighted that highly visual content can travel even without heavy dependence on language.

YouTube said on February 4, 2026 that auto dubbing supports 27 languages and that in December alone more than 6 million daily viewers watched at least 10 minutes of auto-dubbed content.

Sources:

- [2025's biggest trends on YouTube that gave India a new shared vernacular](https://blog.google/intl/en-in/products/platforms/2025s-biggest-trends-on-youtube-that-gave-india-a-new-shared-vernacular/)

Why this matters:

- English-only is unlikely to be enough if scale in India is the target
- bilingual captions and overlays are a high-leverage starting point
- visuals matter because they reduce dependence on language

### Signal 6: India is massive on both Shorts and connected TV

Google India said on September 11, 2025 that India had more than 650 million monthly logged-in Shorts viewers and more than 75 million connected TV viewers.

Source:

- [Brandcast 2025: CTV, Shorts, and YouTube as India's New TV](https://blog.google/intl/en-in/brandcast-2025-ctv-shorts-and-youtube-as-indias-new-tv/)

Why this matters:

## Cricket Audience Baseline

The current ICC growth signals are strong, but the latest explicit ICC demographic baseline I found is older.

ICC said on June 27, 2018 that cricket had more than one billion fans globally in the surveyed 16 to 69 age group, with an average age of 34 and a 61% male / 39% female split. ICC also said 68% of fans were interested in women's cricket and 70% wanted more live coverage of it.

Source:

- [First global market research project unveils more than one billion cricket fans](https://www.icc-cricket.com/media-releases/first-global-market-research-project-unveils-more-than-one-billion-cricket-fans)

This is not current trend data. It is still useful as a baseline:

- cricket fandom is broad, not only male
- the average fan is older than a stereotypical teenage-only assumption
- women's cricket interest has been substantial for years, not just recently

## What This Means for wellBowled.ai

The market signal is not "post more videos."

The real signal is:

- short-form is where attention starts
- visual proof is what earns trust
- creator-style interpretation is what makes sports content watchable
- localization expands reach
- repeatable editorial formats matter more than one-off clips

That leads to a simple strategic rule:

- sell expertise and progress first
- sell product second

### Primary Audience

The first 90-day primary audience should be:

- serious bowlers aged roughly 14 to 30
- cricket-analysis fans who enjoy technical insight

Reason:

- this group is the easiest to hook with proof-led content
- this group is most likely to share with teammates and coaches
- this group is closest to future app usage

### Secondary Audience

The second wave should include:

- coaches and academies
- parents of serious players
- women and girls in cricket via dedicated editorial lanes

### Jobs To Be Done

`wellBowled.ai` content should help the audience do four jobs:

1. Learn one visible bowling insight quickly.
2. Understand why a flaw matters, not just that it exists.
3. Believe that improvement can be measured.
4. Share a credible piece of analysis with someone else.

## Positioning Recommendation

### Recommended Position

`wellBowled.ai` should be positioned as a specialist bowling analysis and coaching brand powered by machine-backed proof.

This positioning has three layers:

- layer 1: coaching insight
- layer 2: visual evidence
- layer 3: product credibility

### What to Avoid

- generic app walkthroughs as the main content format
- vague "AI coach" claims with no visible evidence
- overproduced ad creative that feels less credible than a real coach

### Message Architecture

The strongest message pattern is:

1. state one bowling problem or strength
2. show the evidence
3. explain why it matters
4. give one actionable takeaway
5. reveal the app or system that produced the diagnosis

Example:

- weak: "Our app analyzes your bowling action."
- strong: "Your release is late because your front side opens too early. Freeze it here. This is exactly where you leak pace, and this is how `wellBowled.ai` flags it."

## Strategic Approaches

There are three viable ways `wellBowled.ai` could approach content.

### Approach A: Creator-Coach Brand

What it is:

- a face, voice, or editorial persona explains bowling with visible proof
- the app sits behind the content as the evidence layer

Pros:

- most trust-building
- easiest to share
- strongest bridge to product

Cons:

- requires consistent editorial voice
- depends on someone owning the point of view

Verdict:

- recommended

### Approach B: Product-Led Proof Feed

What it is:

- content mostly shows app screens, app outputs, and feature demos

Pros:

- easy to tie directly to product
- faster to connect to feature releases

Cons:

- weaker discovery
- lower emotional connection
- can feel like advertising

Verdict:

- use as a support lane, not the main lane

### Approach C: Cricket Media Property

What it is:

- broad cricket analysis brand that uses `wellBowled.ai` as one underlying tool

Pros:

- large audience ceiling
- more room for collaborations and sponsorships

Cons:

- easier to drift away from product relevance
- harder to sustain without strong editorial capacity

Verdict:

- possible later, not the first move

## Recommended Editorial Pillars

Start with four pillars, not ten.

### Pillar 1: Technique Proof

Definition:

- one flaw or one strength
- one visible frame
- one explanation

Why it matters:

- easiest to understand
- fastest to produce
- strongest for shares and saves

### Pillar 2: Pro Bowler Deconstruction

Definition:

- analyze what makes a known bowler effective

Why it matters:

- famous names create discoverability
- helps connect expertise to a wider cricket audience

### Pillar 3: App Proof

Definition:

- show how `wellBowled.ai` identifies a useful, specific technique signal

Why it matters:

- converts authority into product interest
- proves the product without making the content feel like an ad

### Pillar 4: Progress and Community

Definition:

- before/after clips
- viewer submissions
- improvement stories

Why it matters:

- strongest social proof
- strongest conversion path

### Dedicated Lane: Women's Fast Bowling

This should run across all four pillars, not sit outside them.

Examples:

- women bowler deconstruction
- women player progress case study
- drill breakdowns tailored to women's cricket pathways

## Recommended Formats

### Format 1: 12-18 second diagnosis clip

Structure:

- 0-1s: hook
- 1-6s: freeze or slo-mo proof
- 6-12s: explanation
- 12-18s: takeaway and CTA

Best for:

- Instagram Reels
- Facebook Reels

### Format 2: 20-35 second micro breakdown

Structure:

- state the claim
- show 2 or 3 proof moments
- explain one key mechanic

Best for:

- YouTube Shorts
- strong Reels when the insight needs a bit more time

### Format 3: Pro vs amateur side-by-side

Structure:

- synchronized frame
- one key difference
- one drill or coaching cue

Best for:

- saves
- shares
- authority

### Format 4: App-backed proof clip

Structure:

- show the raw action
- show what the app flags
- explain why it matters

Best for:

- conversion
- product credibility

### Format 5: Comment-reply video

Structure:

- show a viewer question
- answer with proof

Best for:

- engagement loops
- community depth

### Format 6: Progress journal clip

Structure:

- then vs now
- one measurable change

Best for:

- trust
- retention
- academy and parent audiences

## Creative Principles

The content should feel like modern sports coaching, not generic tech marketing.

Use:

- freeze frames
- clean phase labels
- short caption blocks
- skeleton overlays when they help
- angle lines only when they clarify
- selective slo-mo around gather, stride, and release
- side-by-side synchronization

Avoid:

- cluttered metric dumps
- too many visual layers at once
- generic AI-looking motion graphics
- polished ad voice with low information density

Tone:

- direct
- specific
- credible
- lightly opinionated
- never vague

## 90-Day Operating Plan

### Phase 1: Weeks 1-4

Objective:

- establish visual grammar
- validate the strongest content format
- learn which audience responds fastest

Cadence:

- 4 to 5 short-form posts per week
- 1 deeper YouTube video every 1 to 2 weeks if capacity allows

Mix:

- 40% technique proof
- 25% pro bowler breakdowns
- 20% app proof
- 15% progress or community

Experiments:

- English only vs English plus Hindi text
- text-led hook vs voice-led hook
- pro footage vs owned practice footage

### Phase 2: Weeks 5-8

Objective:

- turn the account from random posting into a recognizable series engine

Actions:

- name recurring series
- reuse templates and overlays
- start comment-reply videos
- launch a women-led recurring lane

Example series:

- `1 Fix in 15 Seconds`
- `Why This Bowler Is Hard To Face`
- `wellBowled.ai Caught This`
- `Your Action vs Pro Action`
- `Women's Fast Bowling Breakdown`

### Phase 3: Weeks 9-12

Objective:

- increase trust and begin pulling viewers toward product and partnerships

Actions:

- viewer clip submissions
- before/after journeys
- coach or academy collaborations
- app proof clips tied to actual outcomes

## Weekly Programming Model

Use a repeatable weekly rhythm so the account feels intentional.

Example:

- Monday: pro bowler deconstruction
- Tuesday: one-fix coaching clip
- Thursday: app proof clip
- Friday: comment-reply or myth-busting clip
- Sunday: progress story or women's bowling feature

This can be adjusted, but the principle is important:

- recurring slots reduce decision fatigue
- recurring slots teach the audience what to expect

## Measurement Framework

Do not optimize for views alone.

Rank success metrics in this order:

1. share rate
2. save rate
3. completion rate
4. qualified comments
5. follows per 1,000 views
6. profile taps
7. site or app clicks
8. inbound submissions or partnership leads

Why:

- shares indicate private utility and social recommendation
- saves indicate educational value
- comments indicate demand for future topics

Review cadence:

- weekly: top clips by share rate and save rate
- monthly: top pillar by conversion behavior
- monthly: top audience segment by response

## Risks and Mitigations

### Risk: the channel becomes a product ad feed

Mitigation:

- keep at least 70% of output audience-first rather than feature-first

### Risk: the content becomes too technical for sharing

Mitigation:

- each clip should teach one idea only

### Risk: automation makes the creative generic

Mitigation:

- keep human editorial judgment at the script stage

### Risk: rights issues around source footage

Mitigation:

- prioritize owned footage, permitted footage, and clearly transformative educational use with caution

### Risk: trying to serve all audiences equally from day one

Mitigation:

- first 90 days should focus on serious bowlers and cricket-analysis fans

## Immediate Decisions Recommended

1. Make Instagram Reels the primary content surface.
2. Make YouTube Shorts the second primary content surface.
3. Cross-post to Facebook Reels as a distribution extension.
4. Do not make TikTok central to the India-first plan.
5. Lead with coaching insight, not product tours.
6. Start with four pillars:
   - technique proof
   - pro bowler deconstruction
   - app proof
   - progress and community
7. Add a dedicated women's bowling lane from the start.
8. Use bilingual captions before scaling full multilingual voice production.
9. Judge content by shares and saves before raw views.
10. Turn formats into named recurring series early.

## Measurement Framework

Do not optimize only for views.

Rank metrics in this order:

1. share rate
2. save rate
3. completion rate
4. comments with real questions
5. follows per 1,000 views
6. profile taps
7. site or app clicks
8. inbound submission or partnership messages

Why:

- share rate indicates private utility and social recommendation
- save rate indicates instructional value
- comments reveal what the audience wants next

Practical review loop:

- weekly review of top 3 clips by share rate
- weekly review of top 3 clips by save rate
- monthly review of which pillar drove the best conversion behavior
