# Speed Gradient — Energy Transfer Model

## Run-Up Phase (Unannotated Feel)

- Whole skeleton warms up uniformly: blue → cyan → green as bowler accelerates toward crease
- No per-joint differentiation — body moves as one unit
- No numbers, no badges, no text overlays
- Pure potential energy buildup coloring

At back foot contact — uniform color **fragments** into per-joint differential colors. The loaded system releases segment by segment.

## Four Transfer Points

Every delivery has 4 energy transfer points in the kinetic chain:

| # | Transfer | What Happens |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **BFC → FFC** | Back foot plants, momentum carries through the stride to front foot |
| 2 | **FFC → Hips** | Front leg braces, ground reaction force drives up into pelvis |
| 3 | **Hips → Trunk** | Hip-shoulder separation unwinds, rotation transfers upward |
| 4 | **Trunk → Arm** | Shoulder pulls bowling arm through the whip |

## Leak Categories (3 Levels Per Transfer)

Every transfer leaks energy — even elite bowlers. The question is how much.

Measured by the frame gap between when one segment peaks and the next segment starts accelerating. Tight gap = clean transfer. Big gap = energy dissipated.

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Minimal leak** | Tight timing — next segment fires right after previous peaks |
| **Moderate leak** | Small delay or overlap, some energy lost |
| **Major leak** | Big gap or wrong ordering, significant energy wasted |

## Visualization Rules

- No numbers during the action
- Skeleton color tells the energy state per joint
- Transfer efficiency shown as link quality between segments (thickness/brightness)
- Fat bright link = most energy got through
- Thin dim link = most leaked away

## Broadcast Limitation

From end-on camera: BFC is reliably visible. FFC can be partially occluded by the body. Front foot contact detection will be unreliable on some deliveries from this angle. Side-on is better for both foot contacts.
