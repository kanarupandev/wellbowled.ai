### What EXISTS in academic research (deep analysis, zero audience)

- [OpenCap bowling 3D analysis (2025)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/17479541251348081) — validated pose estimation vs motion capture for bowling. Lab-grade accuracy, published in a journal nobody reads.
- [Front leg mechanics in fast bowling (PMC 2025)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12899921/) — hip-shoulder separation, front knee angles, ground reaction forces. Dry graphs. Zero visual content.
- [Cricket biomechanics analysis (JPMER)](https://www.jpmer.com/doi/JPMER/pdf/10.5005/jp-journals-10028-1171) — skilled vs amateur fast bowling techniques. 17 key parameters identified. Published as a PDF.
- [Elbow angle measurement systems (ScienceDirect)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0021929007000085) — Vicon vs video accuracy for ICC legality testing. Important work, invisible to fans.

**Pattern:** Deep science, correct data, zero content production. This knowledge sits locked in journals.

## The Three Gaps We Fill

### Gap 1: Kinogram — Nobody has made one for cricket

- Sprint coaches made it the gold standard ([ALTIS](https://altis.world/kinogram-method-ebook/) sells an ebook on the method)
- The principles "can be applied to any movement skill or sport" — ALTIS themselves say this
- A Bumrah kinogram doesn't exist. A Starc kinogram doesn't exist. First mover owns this visual.
- **Opportunity:** The thumbnail that defines the channel. One image = the brand.

### Gap 2: Angle Overlay as Content — Not behind a paywall

- [Quintic](https://www.quinticsports.com/software/) charges for software
- [PaceLab](https://pacelabglobal.com/bespoke/) charges for coaching
- [Labellerr](https://www.labellerr.com/blog/cricket-bowling-analysis-yolov8-pose/) published a tech blog, not a reel
- **Opportunity:** "Bumrah's elbow: 8° — legal" as a 20-second reel. Free, public, shareable. Nobody is doing this.

### Gap 3: Hip-Shoulder Separation Visualized — Everyone talks, nobody shows

- [CricFit](https://www.cricfit.co.uk/post/how-thoracic-mobility-helps-hip-shoulder-separation) writes articles about it
- [JPMER](https://www.jpmer.com/abstractArticleContentBrowse/JPMER/33895/JPJ/fullText) publishes research on it
- [PitchVision](https://www.pitchvision.com/spin-hips) discusses it for spin bowling
- **Opportunity:** Two colored lines on actual bowling footage. Pink hips, cyan shoulders. The rotation gap that generates pace. Nobody has SHOWN this as video content.
