# Top 30 Visual Analysis Elements — What to Show

> The skeleton overlay IS the analysis. Every element below answers one question:
> "What can I show in 1–3 seconds that makes a viewer stop scrolling?"

## The Visual Stack

Each video composites multiple elements from this list. The skeleton is the base layer — everything else builds on top of it.

---

### TIER 1 — The Core (Every Video)

**1. Phase-Colored Skeleton Overlay**
Full-body skeleton with bones/joints color-coded by biomechanical quality.
- Green = textbook mechanics
- Amber = needs attention
- Red = injury risk zone
- Colors shift in real time as the bowler moves through phases
- THIS is the signature visual. No one else has it. It IS the brand.

**2. Phase Label Badge**
Pill overlay showing current bowling phase: "RUN-UP" → "LOADING" → "RELEASE" → "FOLLOW-THROUGH"
- Status dot (green/amber) next to phase name
- Viewer instantly knows what part of the action they're watching
- Creates a visual rhythm — the phases are the story structure

**3. Color Legend Bar**
Bottom-of-screen legend: 🟢 Good · 🟠 Attention · 🔴 Injury Risk
- Teaches first-time viewers the visual language in 1 second
- Becomes iconic brand element across all videos

**4. Key Joint Highlight Dots**
9 biomechanical joints rendered as filled circles with white borders.
Nose, shoulders, hips, knees, ankles — the points that matter for bowling.
- Larger/pulsing dot on the joint being discussed
- White border makes dots pop against any jersey/background color

---

### TIER 2 — The Hooks (High-Impact, Per-Video Choice)

**5. Speed Badge**
Top-left pill: "142 kph" in peacock blue
- The single number every cricket fan understands
- Speed is THE universal metric — it draws even casual fans
- Show speed building through run-up (animated counter)

**6. Bowler DNA Match Card**
Top-right pill: "87% Bumrah" with country flag
- "You bowl like Bumrah" — most shareable single element
- Drives comments: "Do me next!" / "No way that's 87%"
- The name-drop IS the hook for search/discovery

**7. Joint Angle Arc**
Semi-transparent arc drawn at key joints showing exact angle.
- Front knee angle at delivery stride (bracing leg)
- Bowling arm elbow angle (is it legal? the no-ball question)
- Shoulder rotation angle through release
- Numbers overlaid: "162°" in clean sans-serif
- THIS is what separates "analysis" from "just watching"

**8. Freeze Frame + Zoom**
Slow-mo to 0.25x → freeze → 2x zoom on critical joint
- "Look at this knee. 148° — textbook brace. That's where 145 kph comes from."
- The dramatic pause that turns content into teaching
- Best moment: exact release point, front foot landing

**9. Motion Trail / Ghost Path**
Fading trail showing the bowling arm's arc over last 5 frames.
- Visualizes the whip — makes speed feel VISIBLE
- Beautiful: looks like light painting
- Shows wrist snap, shoulder rotation path, follow-through arc

**10. Side-by-Side Skeleton Comparison**
Split screen: two bowlers' skeletons overlaid at the same phase.
- "Bumrah vs Starc at release point"
- Same joint positions, different body shapes — skeleton normalizes everything
- Reveals: who has more shoulder rotation, whose front leg is stiffer
- THE format for "vs" content (infinite combinations)

---

### TIER 3 — The Depth (Makes It Pro-Level)

**11. Force Vector Arrows**
Arrows at key joints showing direction and magnitude of force transfer.
- Ground reaction force through front leg
- Shoulder-to-wrist whip direction
- Hip rotation vector
- Makes invisible physics VISIBLE — the "I never knew that" moment

**12. Kinetic Chain Flow Line**
Glowing line tracing energy transfer: ankle → knee → hip → shoulder → elbow → wrist
- Animated pulse traveling up the chain during delivery
- When the chain breaks (gap in flow) = where efficiency is lost
- Beautiful visualization that simplifies complex biomechanics

**13. Release Point Heat Map**
Over multiple deliveries, plot release points as a heat cloud.
- Tight cluster = consistent bowler
- Scattered = control issue
- "Bumrah's release consistency in the death overs" — analyst catnip

**14. Stride Length Ruler**
Horizontal line from back foot to front foot with measurement.
- "2.4m stride — 91% of body height. Optimal range is 80-100%."
- Simple visual that every bowler can immediately apply to themselves
- Marker on the pitch showing where foot lands

**15. Elbow Flexion Gauge**
Arc gauge around the bowling arm's elbow, like a speedometer.
- 0° = straight arm (legal)
- 15° = ICC limit
- Red zone > 15° (illegal action)
- THE most controversial metric in cricket — guaranteed engagement
- "Is this action legal? The skeleton says..."

**16. Hip-Shoulder Separation Angle**
Two lines — one through hips, one through shoulders — showing the X-factor.
- Animated as bowler rotates through delivery
- Counter showing peak separation: "47° — elite range"
- Directly correlates to pace — every coach knows this

**17. Center of Mass Trajectory**
Dot tracking the body's center of mass through the entire action.
- Smooth line = efficient energy transfer
- Wobble = wasted energy
- Compare elite (smooth curve) vs club bowler (jagged path)

**18. Wrist Snap Velocity Indicator**
Speed gauge on the wrist joint at release moment.
- "Wrist velocity: 28 m/s at release"
- Shows the final multiplier in the kinetic chain
- Animated: accelerates through delivery, peaks at release

---

### TIER 4 — The Engagement Drivers (Shareability + Comments)

**19. Red Zone Pulse**
When a joint enters injury risk zone, the skeleton area PULSES red with a subtle warning glow.
- "Your lower back is screaming at you on every delivery"
- Fear/concern = saves + shares (people tag injured teammates)
- The most emotive element — health > technique

**20. Scoreboard Comparison Bar**
Horizontal bar comparing two bowlers across 5 metrics:
- Pace, accuracy, front leg brace, shoulder rotation, wrist snap
- Like a fighting game character select screen
- "Who wins? Steyn or Akhtar" — debate fuel

**21. Phase Grade Cards**
Letter grade (A+ to D) for each phase with brief verdict.
- Run-Up: A | Loading: B+ | Release: A- | Follow-Through: C
- Gamification — viewers want to know THEIR grade
- Clean, Instagram-card-friendly format

**22. Before/After Split**
Same bowler, two sessions. Left = before coaching. Right = after.
- Skeleton overlay on both
- Delta callouts: "Front knee +12° improvement"
- Proof that analysis leads to improvement — drives app downloads

**23. Slow-Mo + Normal Speed Toggle**
Show delivery at full speed → replay at 0.25x with skeleton
- "This happens in 0.4 seconds. Let's see what your body actually does."
- Speed contrast creates the wow factor
- Full speed shows the raw power; slow-mo shows the science

**24. Timestamp Scrubber**
Thin progress bar at bottom with phase markers.
- Viewer sees exactly where in the delivery they are
- Phase transitions marked with colored dots
- Makes rewatchability obvious — "go back to the release"

---

### TIER 5 — The Polish (Professional Feel)

**25. Bowler Name + Stats Lower Third**
Clean lower-third graphic: "Jasprit Bumrah | IND | 145.2 kph avg | 32 wickets this IPL"
- Broadcast quality — feels like TV production
- Context that makes the analysis meaningful
- Template: flag + name + key stat

**26. wellBowled.ai Watermark**
Subtle bottom-corner logo — peacock blue on semi-transparent black pill.
- Small enough to not annoy, distinctive enough to build brand recall
- Consistent placement across ALL content

**27. Transition Wipe Between Phases**
Quick diagonal wipe or skeleton-outline transition between phases.
- Not just a cut — a designed transition
- Skeleton outline momentarily visible during wipe = brand signature
- 0.3s max — slick, not slow

**28. Audio Sync Markers**
Visual beat markers that sync with:
- Impact sound of front foot landing
- Ball hitting bat/keeper's gloves
- Commentary audio highlights
- Rhythm makes content feel produced, not amateur

**29. Multilingual Caption Burn-In**
Hardcoded captions in English + Tamil/Hindi.
- "Front leg brace angle: 162°" / "முன் கால் கோணம்: 162°"
- Doubles addressable audience instantly
- Accessibility = more shares in non-English cricket markets

**30. QR Code End Card**
Final frame: skeleton pose (frozen at release) + QR code to app/channel.
- "Want YOUR skeleton analyzed? Scan."
- Conversion mechanism built into the content
- The skeleton freeze-frame becomes the channel's thumbnail style

## The Rule

Every frame must answer: **"What is the body doing that the naked eye can't see?"**

The skeleton is the lens. The colors are the verdict. The numbers are the proof.
The viewer leaves knowing something they didn't know before they swiped.
