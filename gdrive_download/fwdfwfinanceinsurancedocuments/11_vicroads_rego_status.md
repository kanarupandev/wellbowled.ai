## URGENT Implications

### 1. Vehicle Cannot Be Driven
The vehicle is currently **unregistered**. It is illegal to drive on public roads in any Australian state. This includes driving it to a Service NSW centre or to a Blue Slip inspection station.

### 2. Insurance May Be Void
The Toyota Insurance policy schedule (02) states the vehicle must be registered. The insurance PDS (05) lists driving an unregistered vehicle as an exclusion. If the vehicle is in an accident while unregistered, the comprehensive insurance claim may be denied.

Additionally, there is **no CTP/TAC cover** while unregistered — if someone is injured, the driver has personal liability.

### 3. Loan Default Risk
The Angle Auto Finance loan general terms (08) require the borrower to:
- Keep the vehicle registered at all times (Part C - Vehicle Obligations)
- Maintain insurance at all times

An unregistered vehicle could be considered a breach of the loan terms and potentially an event of default.

### 4. Blue Slip Validity
The Blue Slip (10) is valid for **42 days from issue**. If the blue slip was issued more than 42 days ago, it has also expired and a new one will be needed. Check the issue date on the physical document.

### 5. Getting the Vehicle to NSW
Since the vehicle cannot be legally driven while unregistered, options are:
- **Tow truck / car carrier** to transport to NSW
- **Temporary VIC permit** — VicRoads may issue a temporary movement permit (unregistered vehicle permit) for a specific journey. Cost ~$50-80.
- **Re-register in VIC first** (renew the expired rego), then drive to NSW, then transfer — but this means paying VIC rego again just to transfer

### 6. No VIC Rego Refund Available
Since the registration has already expired, there is no unexpired period to refund. The earlier estimate of ~12 months refund no longer applies.

1. **Do NOT drive the vehicle** on public roads until registered
2. **Check Blue Slip date** — if still within 42 days, proceed with NSW registration urgently
3. **Contact Angle Auto Finance** (1300 561 868) — inform them of the situation and get consent for NSW registration
