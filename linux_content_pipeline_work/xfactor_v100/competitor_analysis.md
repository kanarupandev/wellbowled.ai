# Competitor Analysis — YouTube Short (5DK2naLACMI)

Channel: Cricket bowling analysis (educational format)
Duration: 58s, 9:16 portrait
Views: Successful channel with engaged audience

## What they do well (learn from)
1. **Face cam circle** (top-right) — personal connection, builds trust
2. **Bold text overlays** — "SEMI OPEN HIP DOMINANT BOWLER", "FAST TWITCH FIBRE", "FRONT FOOT CONTACT" — huge, readable, impossible to miss
3. **High-quality broadcast footage** — match clips, multiple camera angles
4. **Educational pacing** — explains concepts clearly, not rushed
5. **Mixed media** — match footage + nets footage + anatomy diagrams + face cam
6. **Phase labels on the action** — text appears at the right moment
7. **Voice-over driven** — personal narration builds authority

## What they DON'T do (our opportunity)
1. No automated angle measurement — all manual text
2. No real-time tracking lines on the bowler
3. No data-driven verdict/comparison scale
4. No hip/shoulder lines animated on the actual bowler
5. Manual editing — hours per video vs our minutes
6. No quantified comparison (Untrained → Amateur → Good → Elite)
7. No repeatable pipeline — each video is handcrafted

## Our unique advantage
- **Automated measurement** — real angles from pose estimation, not guesswork
- **Visual proof** — the lines on the bowler ARE the evidence
- **Scalable** — minutes per video, not hours
- **Quantified** — comparison scale gives viewers a benchmark
- **Reproducible** — same pipeline on any clip

## What we should adopt
- Bold, huge text (they do this well — our text has been too small)
- Phase labels directly on the action (we do this)
- Consider face cam for future videos (personal connection)
- Consider voice-over for educational depth

## Success metrics to track
- Views per video (benchmark against this channel)
- Watch time / completion rate
- Comments mentioning the angle or comparison
- Shares / screenshots of the verdict card
- Channel subscriber growth rate
