# Content And Footage Strategy

## Content Role

Content is the acquisition engine.
The website is the owned conversion layer.

That means content should be designed to:
- build trust
- demonstrate expertise
- make the visual outputs desirable
- pull visitors into owned channels and paid offers

## Channel Logic

Primary channels:
- YouTube for authority and longer-form breakdowns
- Instagram and Facebook for reach and short-form discovery
- website for owned traffic, capture, offers, and monetization

## IPL Positioning

The business should use IPL as:
- attention engine
- comparison trigger
- seasonal accelerator

It should not become dependent on unsafe clip practices.

## Rights Principle

The business should not rely too heavily on unowned IPL broadcast footage.

Reason:
- takedown risk
- monetization fragility
- platform enforcement risk
- weak business durability

## Better Strategic Framing

Build the brand around:
- elite bowling analysis
- IPL as one major content stream

Not around:
- being an IPL clip account

## Footage Strategy

Best long-term source hierarchy:

### Tier 1
Owned footage
- academy footage
- local footage
- user-submitted footage
- collaboration footage

### Tier 2
Official short-form reference material
- highlights
- official short clips
- public-facing reference assets

### Tier 3
Open or licensed still/reference assets
- editorial stills
- educational or open-license material where appropriate

### Tier 4
Broadcast-dependent material
- use carefully if at all
- do not make this the business foundation

## “Vs IPL Bowler” Strategy

This can still work without owning large amounts of pro footage.

Best version:
- user clip on one side
- transformed elite reference on the other side
- traces, poses, silhouettes, keyframes, timing markers, and comparison models

This is stronger than relying on raw side-by-side broadcast footage.

## Content Rule

**Build a bowling analysis brand that can survive even if IPL clip usage becomes constrained.**
