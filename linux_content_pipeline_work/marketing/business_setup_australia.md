# Business Setup Australia — Simple Starter Approach

## Current Situation

Context:
- located in Cessnock, NSW
- on a Subclass 491 visa
- currently working in daytime as a sole trader with an existing ABN
- remote senior developer work for a Finnish company
- planning to start the bowling-analysis/content venture on the side

## Main Question

Should the new venture start under:
- the same existing sole trader ABN
or
- a new company immediately

## Best Simple Recommendation

**For starters, use the existing sole trader ABN.**

This is the simplest and most practical first move.

## Why This Is The Best Starter Approach

Right now the venture is still in validation mode:
- testing demand
- testing offers
- building content
- proving audience and monetization

A company adds:
- more cost
- more compliance
- more bookkeeping
- more admin

That is usually not the optimal first step if the venture is still being proven.

## Recommended Structure Now

Use the current sole trader ABN for:
- website launch
- content creation
- early pilot sales
- small report/service offers
- initial market validation

## Important Operating Rules

Even if the same ABN is used, the new venture should still be run cleanly.

### 1. Separate bookkeeping
Track the venture separately from the existing contractor work.
At minimum:
- separate categories
- separate spreadsheet or accounting structure
- ideally separate bank account or sub-account for the venture

### 2. Clean records
Keep separate records for:
- income
- expenses
- invoices
- subscriptions
- domains
- service terms
- client work

### 3. Business name if needed
If the venture is publicly trading under a brand such as `wellBowled`, register the relevant business name if required.

### 4. Get advice once
Because of the 491 visa, get proper advice once from:
- a registered migration agent
- an Australian accountant familiar with contractors and small business structures

This should be treated as a confirmation step, not ignored.

## When To Move To A Company

Move to a Pty Ltd later when one or more of these becomes true:
- the venture is making meaningful revenue
- the brand/IP should sit in a separate entity
- larger contracts are being signed
- liability separation becomes important
- staff, contractors, or partners are added
- the business becomes a real operating company instead of an early test

## Tradeoff Summary

### Same ABN now
Pros:
- fastest
- cheapest
- simplest
- least admin
- best for validation stage

Cons:
- no separate legal entity
- liability remains personal
- brand and business are less cleanly separated

### Company now
Pros:
- cleaner structure
- cleaner brand ownership
- better long-term entity setup

Cons:
- more complexity
- more admin
- more cost
- unnecessary too early if the market is not yet proven

## Final Recommendation

**Start under the same ABN.**

But do it in a disciplined way:
- separate tracking
- clean records
- proper advice on visa/compliance
- plan to move to a company later if the venture proves itself

## Bottom Line

The best optimal starter approach is:
1. use the existing ABN
2. keep the venture operationally separate
3. validate demand first
4. move to a company later when revenue or complexity justifies it
