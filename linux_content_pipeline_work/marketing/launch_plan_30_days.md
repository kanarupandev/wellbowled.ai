# Launch Plan — First 30 Days

## Goal

By day 30, the venture should have:
- domains secured
- a simple website live
- one clear starter offer
- strong sample outputs
- content publishing started
- a working lead/enquiry path
- first real market validation through interest, submissions, or customers

## Operating Principle

The purpose of the first 30 days is not perfection.
It is validation.

The founder should optimize for:
- speed
- clarity
- visible quality
- market feedback

Not for:
- full automation
- perfect structure
- full platform buildout

## Week 1 — Foundation

### 1. Secure the brand
- buy `wellBowled.ai`
- buy `wellBowled.com`
- redirect `.com` to `.ai`

### 2. Set up minimum brand infrastructure
- business email
- basic logo or wordmark
- matching handles where possible
- simple shared folder structure for assets and outputs

### 3. Choose the first offer
Launch only one clear starter offer.

Recommended options:
- DNA Signature, if the priority is audience growth and shareability
- basic coach report, if the priority is serious early revenue

Do not launch multiple serious offers at once.

### 4. Set up the working system
Minimum internal setup:
- lead tracker
- content tracker
- reference clip bank
- output templates
- customer notes template

Simple tools are enough.

### 5. Confirm compliance once
Because the founder is on a 491 visa and already trading under an ABN:
- get one migration/accounting sanity check
- confirm the starter structure is acceptable
- then move on

## Week 2 — Productization

### 6. Build 3 to 5 sample outputs
These should be good enough to publish or show to prospects.

Suggested mix:
- one DNA Signature sample
- one elite comparison sample
- one extracted-figure analysis sample
- one coach-report style sample page

### 7. Build the website skeleton
Minimum pages:
- Home
- How it works
- Offer
- About
- Contact / enquiry

Optional:
- sample breakdowns
- submit-your-clip page
- waitlist page

### 8. Write the offer clearly
The offer page should explain:
- what it is
- who it is for
- what input is required
- what output is delivered
- turnaround time
- price or beta-positioning
- clear call to action

### 9. Create intake flow
Prospects should be able to do one clear thing:
- enquire
- submit a clip
- join a waitlist

Make the path easy.

## Week 3 — Audience Launch

### 10. Publish the site
Do not wait for perfection.
A simple and strong website is enough.

### 11. Publish the first content batch
Suggested minimum:
- 2 YouTube ideas or videos in progress
- 5 to 10 short-form posts or clips
- 1 website article or analysis page

### 12. Use strong starter content themes
Suggested early themes:
- who do you bowl like
- what makes this elite bowler unique
- seam bowling phase breakdowns
- amateur vs elite differences
- visual action comparisons

### 13. Attach a conversion action to content
Every content piece should support one of:
- submit your clip
- join waitlist
- enquire for report
- visit website
- follow for more breakdowns

## Week 4 — Validation

### 14. Get real user inputs
Target:
- 5 to 10 real clips
- from friends, local bowlers, coaches, or online outreach

### 15. Deliver manually if needed
This is acceptable.
The goal is to learn what buyers respond to, not to prove full automation.

### 16. Observe what people care about
Watch for:
- what they ask first
- what they share
- what they pay for
- what they ignore
- what confuses them
- which output feels strongest

### 17. Tighten the offer
After a few real cases:
- simplify weak parts
- strengthen the strongest output
- tighten scope
- improve delivery wording
- adjust pricing if needed

### 18. Decide the next step
At the end of day 30, choose whether to:
- keep the same offer
- add a second offer
- improve quality further
- increase publishing volume
- formalize structure later if traction justifies it

## What Not To Do In The First 30 Days

- do not build the full platform
- do not launch too many offers
- do not overdesign the website
- do not delay launch for company setup
- do not try to support every bowling type
- do not confuse views with real validation

## Best Outcome By Day 30

A successful first 30 days means:
- the brand is live
- the site is live
- the first offer is understandable
- sample outputs look premium
- real clips are coming in
- the founder knows what people respond to

That is enough.

## Core Rule

**The first 30 days are for proving demand, not proving completeness.**
