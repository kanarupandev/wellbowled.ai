# Brand Strategy

## Current Naming Question

The strategic naming question is whether the company brand should be:
- broad enough to hold multiple bowling products
or
- narrow and feature-specific around seam/DNA/analysis

## Brand Conclusion

The umbrella brand should stay broader than any single feature.

Reason:
- cricket is already a niche
- bowling is already a narrow enough vertical
- the company is likely to expand beyond one feature

That means the company brand should be able to hold:
- DNA Signature
- extracted-figure analysis
- coach reports
- clip management
- future software and workflows
- future expansion into spin later if desired

## Why Narrow Feature Names Are Weaker As Company Names

Names like:
- BowlDNA
- SpellDNA
- PaceGlyph
- SeamLab

may work well as:
- product names
- module names
- internal frameworks

But they are weaker as umbrella brands because they narrow the company too early.

## Current Brand Recommendation

Current strongest umbrella-brand direction:
- `wellBowled.ai` as primary
- `wellBowled.com` redirecting to `.ai`

Why it works:
- cricket-native
- brandable
- broad enough for future products
- strong enough for content/media
- flexible enough for services and future software

## Honest Tradeoff

Main weakness of `wellBowled`:
- less immediately descriptive as analysis software than a narrow technical name

Why that is acceptable:
- this business is not only software
- it is also content, media, identity products, services, and future tooling

## Recommended Brand Architecture

Umbrella brand:
- `wellBowled`

Example future sub-products:
- `wellBowled DNA`
- `wellBowled Analysis`
- `wellBowled Coach Report`
- `wellBowled Compare`
- `wellBowled Seam Lab`

## Naming Rule

**Use narrower names at the product layer, not at the company layer.**
