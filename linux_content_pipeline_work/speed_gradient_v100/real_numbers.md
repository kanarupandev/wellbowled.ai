# Real Numbers — From Research

## Segment Contribution to Ball Release Speed

Source: Zhang et al. (3D kinematic analysis, published in J Biomech)

| Segment | Max Effort | Earlier Study |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| Run-up | 8% | 16% |
| Pelvis rotation | 12% | 2% |
| Trunk rotation + flexion | 29% | 6% |
| Arm circumduction | 48% | 62% |
| Forearm + hand | 3% | 14% |

**Key takeaway: Arm contributes ~48-62%, trunk ~6-29%, pelvis ~2-12%.**
The variation between studies shows technique differences matter hugely.

## Other Key Numbers

- Ground reaction force at delivery: **6-8× body weight**
- Non-bowling arm contribution: **up to 13%** of ball speed
- Wrist snap addition: **5-10%** of ball speed
- Run-up optimal speed: **17-21 km/h**
- Front knee angle at FFC (optimal): **170-175°**

## What This Means for Our Pipeline

The transfer efficiency between segments should follow:
- Pelvis → Trunk: trunk should contribute ~2-4× what pelvis contributes
- Trunk → Arm: arm should contribute ~1.5-2× what trunk contributes
- Arm → Wrist: wrist adds 5-10% on top

If the ratio drops (e.g., arm only contributes equal to trunk), energy is leaking.

We measure VELOCITY not energy, but the RATIO between segments should follow similar patterns since kinetic energy ∝ velocity².
