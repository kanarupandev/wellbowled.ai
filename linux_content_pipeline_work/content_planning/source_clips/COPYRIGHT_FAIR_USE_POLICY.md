# Copyright & Fair Use Policy — wellBowled.ai Content

## Position

All source clips used in wellBowled.ai content are short excerpts from publicly available footage, used under fair use / fair dealing for the purpose of education, commentary, and analysis.

## Fair Use Protections Applied

1. **Short excerpts only** — each clip used is 2-5 seconds maximum.
2. **Heavily transformed** — skeleton overlays, pose analysis, side-by-side comparison, colour grading. The original footage is not presented as-is.
3. **Educational / commentary purpose** — the content exists to analyse bowling mechanics, not to replay cricket entertainment.
4. **No substitute for the original** — a 3-second overlaid clip does not replace watching the original match or video.
5. **Credit given** — sources are acknowledged.

## What This Means in Practice

- YouTube Content ID claims are expected and accepted. Ad revenue on claimed segments may go to the original rights holder.
- Takedown strikes are unlikely for heavily transformed analytical content but remain a possibility.
- No broadcast match footage is used where avoidable. Preference is for nets/practice footage and fan-uploaded Shorts.

## If a Claim or Strike Occurs

1. Do not dispute Content ID claims — accept revenue sharing.
2. If a takedown strike is received, remove the clip and replace with an alternative source.
3. Long-term: source original footage by filming bowlers at nets with own camera.

## Safest Long-Term Path

Film original footage at nets sessions. This eliminates all copyright risk and creates a unique content moat. Current YouTube-sourced clips are a bootstrap strategy for the first content pieces only.
