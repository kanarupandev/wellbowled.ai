# Bumrah vs Steyn: Why Bumrah Feels Wrong... But Still Works

## Status

Selected as the first flagship content piece.

## Final Working Title

`Bumrah vs Steyn: Why Bumrah Feels Wrong... But Still Works`

## Why This Is The Right First Video

- Bumrah gives immediate curiosity and recognizability.
- Steyn provides the clean textbook reference point.
- The side-by-side creates obvious visual contrast.
- The topic expresses the wellBowled philosophy clearly:
  different does not mean broken.
- The idea can scale into a repeatable series:
  - Bumrah vs Steyn
  - Malinga vs textbook
  - Anderson vs express pace bowlers
  - Starc vs classical left-arm action

## Core Idea

Two elite fast bowlers.
Very different actions.
Same bowling problem solved in different ways.

## Core Takeaway

Different is not broken.
Elite bowlers do not all need to look the same.

## What The Video Must Do

1. Show why Bumrah feels visually unusual.
2. Show Steyn as the classical contrast.
3. Make the viewer see the difference immediately.
4. Reframe that difference as elite variation, not error.
5. Leave the viewer with a stronger mental model of bowling actions.

## What The Video Must Not Try To Do

- prove exact biomechanics
- tell viewers to copy Bumrah
- prescribe a coaching fix
- turn into a legality/chucking debate
- overwhelm the viewer with numbers

## Audience Outcome

The viewer should leave thinking:

- "Now I can see why Bumrah looks so different."
- "Different actions can still be elite."
- "wellBowled shows bowling in a clearer way than normal cricket content."

## Next Stage

Move from `why` to `what`:

- what the video actually is
- what scenes it contains
- what comparison moments it must show
- what single idea each scene carries
