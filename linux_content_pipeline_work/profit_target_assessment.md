# Profit Target Assessment — 120k AUD Profit in 2 Years

## Decision

**Verdict: plausible, but not by treating this as a pure engineering project.**

Target:
- `120,000 AUD` profit within `2 years`
- Equivalent to roughly `60,000 AUD` profit per year

For a niche cricket business built around bowling analysis + content creation, this is credible.

It is not easy.
It is not likely by accident.
It requires commercial discipline much more than extra pipeline sophistication.

## Current Advantage

The founder profile materially improves the odds:
- senior AI engineer
- extensive cricket knowledge
- able to judge whether outputs are genuinely useful in cricket terms
- able to build internal tooling faster and cheaper than most competitors
- able to create differentiated cricket analysis content rather than generic AI output

This combination is a real advantage.

## Main Risk

The main risk is **not** technical failure.

The main risk is building a technically impressive system before proving:
- who pays
- what exact outcome they pay for
- how often they pay
- what price level supports real profit

Pipeline quality, segmentation quality, and biomechanical correctness matter.
They are not the business by themselves.

## Core Commercial Reality

People may not pay for:
- accurate segmental kinetic-chain analysis
- elegant pipeline architecture
- technically correct internal manifests

They may pay for:
- "Why is my pace stuck?"
- "Give me a coach-ready breakdown in 24 hours"
- "Compare me to elite bowlers"
- "Turn my clip into premium cricket content"
- "Help my academy create analysis and social media at scale"

That distinction matters.

## Best Interpretation Of The Current Effort

The current technical work is valuable if treated as:
- an internal leverage engine
- a content production engine
- a service delivery engine
- a credibility engine

It is less attractive if treated as:
- a generic self-serve AI app first
- a broad horizontal sports-tech platform
- a product that must be fully automated before revenue starts

## Most Plausible Business Paths

### 1. Service first, software second
This is the most credible path.

Approach:
- deliver bowling analysis manually or semi-automatically
- use the pipeline internally to improve speed and quality
- charge early
- productize only repeated, paid-for parts later

Why this path is strong:
- fastest route to revenue
- tests willingness to pay early
- avoids overbuilding
- lets the tooling mature behind real customer demand

### 2. Content engine with paid upsell
Approach:
- publish compelling bowling breakdowns
- grow authority and trust
- monetize through premium reports, memberships, coaching products, or academy packages

Why this path is strong:
- your cricket knowledge improves content credibility
- your engineering gives you a visual/content moat
- content becomes customer acquisition, not just marketing overhead

### 3. Narrow B2B cricket tooling
Approach:
- sell to academies, specialist coaches, pathways, or cricket media/content operators

Why this path is plausible:
- fewer customers needed
- higher ticket potential
- stronger retention if embedded in workflow

Why it is harder:
- slower sales cycle
- requires clearer ROI story

## What Is Not Plausible

Less plausible paths:
- building a polished technical product first and assuming revenue will follow
- delaying monetization until full automation exists
- assuming "AI bowling analysis" alone is enough of a moat
- trying to serve all cricket users at once

## My Honest View

With this founder profile, `120k AUD profit in 2 years` is **credible**.

But only if the business is run like this:
- sell outcomes, not pipeline sophistication
- validate willingness to pay quickly
- use the tool internally before forcing it into full product form
- choose one customer segment first
- let content or services pull the roadmap

If the project remains primarily an engineering build, I would not expect the target to be likely.

If it becomes a focused content + service + narrow product business, I would call the target realistic enough to pursue seriously.

## Short Strategic Conclusion

### Strongest assets
- technical leverage
- domain credibility
- differentiated cricket content potential
- ability to iterate without outside dependency

### Biggest danger
- overinvesting in technical completeness before proving commercial demand

### Most likely route to 120k profit
- service-first delivery
- content-led acquisition
- narrow paid offers
- internal tooling advantage
- selective productization later

## Operating Rule

**Do not wait for full automation to test the market.**

Revenue and market proof should come before pipeline perfection.
