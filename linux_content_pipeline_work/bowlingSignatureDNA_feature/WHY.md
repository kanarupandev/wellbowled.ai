# WHY

## Why This Feature Exists

The Bowling Signature DNA feature exists because bowling analysis becomes much more valuable when it answers a human question, not only a biomechanical one.

The human question is:
- who or what do I bowl like?

That question is powerful because it creates:
- identity
- curiosity
- shareability
- explainability
- coaching relevance

## Business Why

This feature solves several business problems at once.

### 1. Strong acquisition hook
People care more about:
- “Who do I bowl like?”
than:
- “Your trunk flexion timing is delayed.”

That makes this feature a strong top-of-funnel product.

### 2. Premium analysis bridge
The feature is not just entertainment.
If designed well, it becomes a bridge between:
- consumer-friendly identity outputs
and
- coach-friendly technical interpretation

### 3. Data moat
The long-term value is not just in the UI.
It is in building:
- a bowling ontology
- a phase model
- a parameter taxonomy
- a canonical bowler signature library
- a retrieval and clustering system

That becomes proprietary structure.

## Product Why

The purpose is not to do celebrity matching for its own sake.
The real purpose is to build an archetype system.

That means the system should answer:
- what action family is this user closest to?
- which elite references best represent this action style?
- where does the user align with that archetype?
- where does the user differ?

This makes the result useful as:
- an identity output
- a coaching reference
- a comparison engine
- a content engine

## Coaching Why

Bowling actions are complex.
A coach-friendly system needs a way to compress that complexity into:
- phases
- parameters
- signature families
- nearest archetypes

That is exactly what the DNA system is for.

The point is not to replace coaching judgment.
The point is to make coaching judgment easier to structure and communicate.

## Why Archetypes Matter More Than Exact Ground Truth

Ordinary user video is not motion-capture.
So the feature should not pretend it can recover perfect 3D biomechanics from every clip.

What it can do well is:
- classify visible action structure
- describe action family
- identify nearest archetypes
- explain why the match was made

That is a better and more robust product problem.

## Why The Match Must Be Based On Action Signature

If the system relies too much on fields such as:
- total wickets
- ICC ranking
- highest career speed
- awards

then it stops being a bowling-signature engine and becomes a biographical similarity engine.

That would be the wrong product.

The primary match should be based on:
- phased action descriptors
- release family
- run-up/gather structure
- front arm / front leg / trunk / follow-through signatures
- a few normalized quantitative features where reliable

## Why This Is Feasible

It is feasible because elite bowlers often have strongly visible action signatures.
For example, bowlers such as:
- Lasith Malinga
- Jasprit Bumrah
- Mitchell Starc
- Josh Hazlewood

are not only statistically different.
They are visually and structurally different in action shape.

That means archetype retrieval is feasible even when exact biomechanics are not perfectly measurable.

## Long-Term Why

The long-term purpose is to build a bowler-archetype system with roughly 1000 clusters.

Some clusters may contain:
- several international bowlers with closely related signatures

Some clusters may contain:
- a single highly unique bowler

That is acceptable.
The point is not to force all bowlers into broad groups.
The point is to map the bowling-action landscape in a structured, retrievable way.

## Final Why Statement

The Bowling Signature DNA feature exists to turn a bowling video into a structured archetype match that is useful for:
- identity
- coaching
- explainability
- content
- long-term productization
